How to Maintain Your Cedar Fence for 20+ Years

By David Ross • 8 Min Read • Mar 15, 2026

Western Red Cedar is naturally rot-resistant, but it's not invincible. The UV rays from the sun and the fluctuating humidity of the Midwest can cause even the best lumber to grey and warp over time if left unprotected.

1. The Cleaning Phase

Before applying any stain, the wood must be porous. We recommend an oxygen-based cleaner (not bleach) to lift deep-seated dirt and mill glaze without damaging the cellulose fibers of the wood.

2. Choosing the Right Stain

We exclusively use oil-based penetrating stains. Unlike water-based options that sit on top like paint, oil-based stains soak into the wood, replenishing the natural oils and providing a moisture barrier from the inside out.
